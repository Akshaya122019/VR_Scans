COMMON TYPES OF ULTRASOUND SCANS

Ultrasound scans use high-frequency sound waves to create detailed images of the body's internal structures. They are safe, painless, and radiation-free, making them ideal for diagnosing a variety of medical conditions.

Abdominal Ultrasound

Examines the liver, kidneys, pancreas, gallbladder, spleen, and abdominal blood vessels. Helps detect gallstones, liver disease, kidney stones, and abdominal masses

Pelvic Ultrasound

Used to assess the uterus, ovaries, bladder, and prostate gland. Helps diagnose ovarian cysts, fibroids, urinary disorders, and reproductive health issues.

KUB Ultrasound

A KUB ultrasound, or kidney, ureter, and bladder ultrasound, is a non-invasive imaging technique that examines the urinary tract.

Doppler Ultrasound

Evaluates blood flow in arteries and veins. Helps detect blood clots, deep vein thrombosis (DVT), and blockages.

Echocardiography

Assesses the heart's structure, valves, and blood flow. Used to detect heart diseases, valve disorders, and congenital heart conditions.

Thyroid Ultrasound

Examines the thyroid gland to check for nodules, enlargement, or tumors. Helps diagnose thyroid disorders such as goiter or thyroiditis.
